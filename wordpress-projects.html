Why WordPress for client projects?

WordPress is flexible, extendable, and well supported — that’s why I choose it for most client projects. With the right combination of a lightweight theme, targeted plugins, and clean code, you can deliver fast, SEO-optimized websites that are simple for clients to update.

My workflow (how I work on WordPress projects)

  1. Requirement gathering: Understand client goals and the primary user actions (contact, book, buy).
  2. Design & prototypes: Quick Figma mockups or Elementor wireframes for layout sign-off.
  3. Development: Lightweight starter theme, use child theme for custom styles, and optimize assets.
  4. SEO & performance: Proper heading usage, meta tags, image optimization, and caching.
  5. Testing & handover: Cross-browser test, mobile testing, and a simple instruction guide for the client.

Key lessons & recommendations

After building multiple WordPress sites, these are the things I repeat on every project:

  • Speed matters: Optimize images, use webp where possible, and limit heavy plugins.
  • Use schema: Article, Organization, LocalBusiness schemas help Google better understand content.
  • Content-first: Build templates around content: clear headings, short paragraphs, and internal linking.
  • Backups & staging: Use staging site and schedule backups before every change.
